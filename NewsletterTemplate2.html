Hot Topics

1

Do you share your research plans with executives?

Florian Fiechter asked:


“Have research plans been effective or not in getting executive air cover?”

They go on to ask what has worked well for you when writing and sharing research plans with executives, if you have done that before.

  • Julie Eichstaedt confirmed that not only had they had success with sharing research plans, but also that those plans should be shared with anyone in your organization who would benefit from those plans. They continued with the advice to keep the plan the length and depth of an Executive Summary.

  • Watan Raj added a simple tip to always ask for half again more than the required timeline, and constructing the research plan in connected stages, by which they mean planning to the second part of the process before getting close to completing the first part.

Share your perspective.

2

Who should manage an embedded UX team?

Annette Greiner wants to know:


“Does the manager of an embedded team need to be someone other than a UX person?”


They are looking for advice on what to aim for when it comes to growing their own division's embedded team.

  • Gabriela Veghova recommended a UX Manager as a good candidate to build and manage an embedded team. “They have enough autonomy and power to set up a reasonable design practice within the division.”

What do you think?

3

How frequently should team members observe users?

Emily Ennis asked:


”Does anyone in the community have tips for keeping up a regular cadence for live observation/interviews while also sharing observations in a meaningful and timely way?”


  • Kevin Luddy said that they were also exploring this idea and they had considered setting office hours for internal users but were also looking at adding an interview signup link to their qualtrics feedback popup. 

  • Jared Spool also pointed out that sharing observations isn’t as effective as having people observe for themselves.

Add your thoughts.
